Strengths & Weaknesses

Tap any point below to see what it looks like in practice.

Strengths

Rapid decision-making
Makes decisions quickly without delay, which is critical in crisis management.
Clear command structure
Provides clear, direct instructions, reducing confusion among team members.
Control and authority
Centralized control allows enforcement of rules and order in volatile situations.

Weaknesses

Limited input
Rarely seeks team perspectives, which can create blind spots.
Employee disengagement
Team members may feel demoralized with no voice in decisions.
Over-reliance on one perspective
May miss critical insights from subject matter experts.

Rudy Giuliani
Mayor of New York City · 9/11 Crisis Response

Context

During the 9/11 attacks, Giuliani used an autocratic approach to provide immediate direction and maintain order amid the crisis.

Effectiveness

His decisive coordination of emergency response, clear public instructions, and visible leadership presence were widely praised.

Limitation

The autocratic approach suited the immediate crisis but created challenges in later collaborative recovery efforts.
